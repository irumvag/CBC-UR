## Best Practices by Phase

### Planning Phase
- Begin venue booking **at least 6–8 weeks** in advance for large events
- Submit all permit applications with a minimum **4-week lead time**
- Create a detailed run-of-show document with minute-by-minute timing
- Establish a clear budget with a **10–15% contingency buffer**
- Identify and brief a dedicated day-of coordinator

### Promotion Phase
- Launch promotion **3–4 weeks** before the event
- Use a multi-channel approach: email, social media, flyers, campus screens
- Partner with student organizations to amplify reach
- Send reminder communications at 1 week and 48 hours before the event
- Create an event hashtag for social engagement

### Execution Phase
- Conduct a full venue walkthrough **24 hours** before the event
- Brief all volunteers and staff with a printed run-of-show
- Designate a single point of contact for day-of vendor coordination
- Keep a real-time issue log during the event
- Have emergency contacts (facilities, security, medical) on speed dial

### Post-Event Phase
- Send thank-you emails to speakers, sponsors, and volunteers within **48 hours**
- Distribute attendee feedback surveys within **24 hours** while experience is fresh
- Compile attendance data, budget actuals, and media coverage
- Hold a debrief meeting with the core team within **1 week**
- Document lessons learned and update templates for future events

## Guiding Principles

**Inclusivity First**
Design every event so that all students — regardless of ability, background, or financial situation — can participate meaningfully.

**Student Ownership**
Empower student organizations to lead. My role is to facilitate, not to take over. The best events feel like they belong to the students.

**Data-Driven Improvement**
Every event generates data. Attendance numbers, feedback scores, and budget variances all inform how we do better next time.

**Sustainability**
Minimize single-use materials, partner with campus sustainability offices, and choose local vendors where possible.

**Safety Above All**
No event outcome — attendance records, sponsor satisfaction, or departmental expectations — is worth compromising the safety of any attendee.
